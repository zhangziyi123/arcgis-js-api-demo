Summarizing Data

To better understand spatial relationships in data, we can summarize features that meet specified spatial criteria. These tools allow total counts, lengths, areas and attributes to be summarized by defined areas.

For example, we may want to better understand what is happening in different areas (e.g. the total green space within different cities) or what is happening nearby (e.g. area of each vegetation type in the 5-20m riparian zone from the river) or even simply learn how much there is within an area (e.g. schools within a neighborhood).

Solutions:
Aggregate Points
Summarize Nearby
Summarize Within

Aggregate Points

Aggregate points allows you to aggregate or count the total number of points that fall within specified polygons or areas. Additionally, you can calculate various statistics such as Sum, Mean, Min, Max, etc for attributes contained in your points layer.
